Built on the Rock
Stepping Into a Year of God’s Foundation

Key Scripture

“Unless the Lord builds the house, the builders labor in vain.”

Psalm 127:1 (NIV)

As 2025 came to a close, the ministry paused to remember God’s unmistakable faithfulness. What began as small acts of obedience grew into thriving outreach efforts—food distribution, a second ministry launch, over 250 volunteers stepping forward, and a Christmas street outreach that touched the forgotten and unseen. Yet as the calendar turned toward 2026, the Spirit made something clear: this would be a year of fixing, strengthening, and securing the foundation for what’s coming next.

Testimony

Through prayer and reflection, it became evident that God had been aligning pieces like a divine puzzle. What looked scattered in former seasons was suddenly forming clarity. Growth had come quickly—so quickly that the team realized the foundation needed reinforcement before expansion continued. God wasn’t slowing them down; He was strengthening what would carry the weight of the next season.

With that revelation came conviction about Sabbath rest, healthy delegation, and strengthening leadership. The call wasn’t to strive harder but to trust deeper. Just as Mary quietly carried a promise she didn’t fully understand, this season required quiet faith—believing God in the unseen, saying “yes” even when the process felt uncomfortable or stretching.

There was also personal healing God continued to walk them through—deliverance, discipleship, and obedience that reshaped identity. Setbacks that once felt like failure were revealed as God’s protection, His way of redirecting footsteps to safer ground. The Lord also highlighted how noise—internal and external—had been drowning out His voice. This year demanded intentional quiet, repentance, and renewed intimacy with Jesus. And as always, the invitation remained open for every believer to live boldly, serve passionately, evangelize fearlessly, and walk into the calling God had prepared.

What God Revealed

  • Rapid growth must be anchored in strong spiritual foundations.
  • Sabbath rest and delegation are not weaknesses—they are obedience.
  • Setbacks can be divine protection rather than punishment.
  • Quiet faith—like Mary’s—is often the birthplace of God’s greatest works.
  • Obedience, not comfort, shapes the disciples God uses.

“What feels like a setback may actually be God pulling you into the safety of His will.”

Reflection

You may find yourself in a season where God is rebuilding, pruning, or slowing things down. It might feel uncomfortable, but be encouraged—He strengthens what He intends to expand. If you’ve been wrestling with doubt, exhaustion, or confusion, know this: God sees you. He is not disappointed in your struggle. He is shaping you into someone who can carry His promises with boldness and maturity. Let Him rebuild your foundation. Let Him speak into the quiet places of your heart. His plans for you have not wavered.

Call to Action

If you feel far from Jesus, come home. Repent. Ask Him to wash you clean and restore your heart. Step into community. Serve where He leads. Share your testimony boldly. Don’t let fear silence you—God placed His Spirit inside you for a purpose. This is your year to walk with courage, to evangelize, to love radically, and to pursue a faith that is active, not passive.

Closing Prayer

“Lord, thank You for strengthening my foundation. Remove every trace of doubt and remind me that You have equipped me for what lies ahead. Pour out Your Spirit on my life in fresh ways. Use me boldly in 2026, and let my life reflect Your glory. Amen.”
