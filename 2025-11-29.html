From Chains to Calling
A Story of Redemption and Purpose

Key Scripture

“Therefore if anyone is in Christ, the new creation has come: The old has gone, the new is here.”

2 Corinthians 5:17 (NIV)

This testimony traces a powerful journey from addiction and loss into a renewed identity in Christ, shaped through Christian hip-hop ministry, God-led restoration, and a calling realized through community and evangelism.

Testimony

Years of addiction left him broken and wandering, carrying wounds from loss and the weight of choices that stole his peace. The world had stripped him of identity, but God was not finished with his story. In the darkest places, grace began to break through.

As he encountered Christian hip-hop, the lyrics became lifelines. Community formed around him—brothers who spoke life instead of judgment. Slowly, God rebuilt what addiction had torn apart. The pain became testimony, the wounds became ministry.

Faith Jam became a turning point, a place where God clarified his calling. What began as survival transformed into purpose, leading to the “Day of Redemption” event in Panama City—a proclamation that no life is too far for Jesus to restore.

What God Revealed

  • God restores what addiction tries to destroy.
  • Community is a lifeline, not a luxury.
  • Your story becomes healing when surrendered to Christ.
  • Calling often emerges from the deepest wounds.
  • Jesus never stops pursuing the broken.

“God didn’t just pull me out—He sent me back with purpose.”

Reflection

If you feel lost, addicted, or ashamed, know this: God is still writing your story. What feels like ruin can become redemption. Jesus meets you in the mess, not after you clean it up. You are not alone, and your future is not determined by your past.

Call to Action

Turn toward Jesus today. Reach out for prayer, step into community, and choose the path of restoration. And if you're near Panama City Beach, come be part of the “Day of Redemption” concert—an atmosphere of hope, healing, and revival.

Closing Prayer

“Lord Jesus, redeem every broken place in us. Restore what was lost, heal what was wounded, and lead us into the calling You prepared. Amen.”
