When God Confirms the Calling
Even Through the Fire

Key Scripture

“With man this is impossible, but with God all things are possible.”

Matthew 19:26 (NIV)

The Salty Farm Ministries team returned from Austin with full hearts and fresh vision after witnessing a tiny‑home community designed to restore dignity to the unhoused. It became a catalyst for the dream God birthed in them—feeding the hungry, offering mobile showers, and building a future tiny‑home village where broken lives can be rebuilt through the love of Jesus.

Testimony

After returning from Austin, the team shared their excitement and vision. But what began as conversation quickly felt like doubt—questions that sounded more like challenges. Their hearts sank under the weight of discouragement. They had expected unity, but instead felt misunderstood and spiritually attacked.

As they prayed, God showed them the truth: the team wasn’t attacking. They were simply earlier in the journey. Leaders are called to model grace, to slow down, to explain the vision, and to trust God—not to strive. The dream of land and funding wasn’t on their shoulders. God had already been opening doors, sending unexpected “angels,” and stirring the hearts of millionaires and even billionaires who wanted to help. Provision had never been the issue—perspective was.

Then Sunday came. Their pastor preached on Mary—how she asked “How will this be?” but never questioned “if.” Her obedience echoed through their weary spirits. And then, unexpectedly, the pastor affirmed them publicly, declaring God’s calling over their ministry. In that moment, the weight lifted. The team was restored, unity returned, and discouragement broke. What the enemy meant for division, God turned into confirmation.

What God Revealed

  • Leaders must extend grace, even when questions feel like doubt.
  • Unity is strengthened through humility, patience, and clear communication.
  • Provision comes from God, not from human striving—He sends the right people at the right time.
  • Faith grows when we replace “if” with “how,” trusting God’s plan.
  • Trials refine the calling and expose the enemy’s attempts to divide.

“God never asked you to make it happen—only to say yes and trust Him to finish the work.”

Reflection

Maybe you’ve felt misunderstood, discouraged, or attacked right in the middle of trying to follow God. Maybe your “yes” led you straight into a storm. You’re not alone. God often confirms the calling only after the testing. Let this testimony remind you that the enemy attacks what he fears—and he fears your obedience. Hold steady. Listen for God’s whisper. Your Sunday is coming too.

Call to Action

Pray for the ministry, pray for unity, and pray for courage to obey your own calling. Support the mission—serve, give, or even round up at Ace Hardware to help build what God has started. Say yes to Jesus today. He will always meet you with grace and power.

Closing Prayer

“Lord, strengthen my faith. Help me say yes to Your calling even when I don’t understand the how. Unite our hearts, protect our mission, and let Your provision and presence lead us every step of the way. Amen.”
